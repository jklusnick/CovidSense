According to the Center for Disease Control a temperature reading of 100.4° Fahrenheit (38° Celsius) or above indicates a fever.
A patient is considered ill if their fever persists for at least 48 hours or is accompanied by one or more of the following:
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Persistent cough
  • Persistent vomiting
  • Persistent diarrhea
  • Headache with stiff neck
  • Confusion or decreased consciousness
  • Skin rash
  • New unexplained bruising or bleeding
